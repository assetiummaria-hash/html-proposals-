01 / The Complete Model

How the ARDEWA Basket Works

The ARDEWA Basket is a regulated, institutional-grade tokenization structure that allows a Luxembourg SPV to contribute the economic rights of its underlying European assets to a regulated cross-border vehicle in Dubai, without selling the assets, without conventional debt, and without losing beneficial ownership. The model is built on a double-vehicle legal architecture that separates the jurisdiction of the asset (Luxembourg) from the jurisdiction of the issuance (Dubai, under VARA Rulebook 2.0). This separation is the structural element that makes cross-border tokenization legally sound in both jurisdictions simultaneously.

At its core, the model has three layers. The first layer is the Luxembourg SPV, which retains full legal title to the underlying assets at all times. Title never leaves Europe. The SPV signs an Assignment of Economic Rights Agreement with the DIFC Prescribed Company in Dubai, contractually assigning the rights over rental income, asset appreciation, or other revenue streams. A security interest is perfected under Luxembourg law, and an independent legal opinion from Luxembourg counsel confirms cross-border enforceability.

The second layer is the DIFC Prescribed Company (PCR 2024) in Dubai, constituted with Qualifying Purpose: Structured Financing. The DIFC PC is the creditor of the Luxembourg SPV and the holder of the assigned economic rights. It acts as the bridge between the European asset layer and the Dubai-regulated token layer.

The third layer is the regulated issuance and distribution of ARVA tokens. Against the economic rights held by the DIFC PC, a VARA-licensed Third-Party Tokenization Provider mints the ARVA tokens on the XRP Ledger. Each token represents a creditor position in the DIFC PC, entitling the holder to a pro-rata share of the economic rights held by the DIFC PC over the Luxembourg SPV's assets. A VARA-licensed Custodian provides institutional-grade custody of the tokens. Regulated Distributors place the tokens with pre-vetted institutional investors.

02 / The Verification Layer

Assetium: Without This, the Model Does Not Function

There is a fundamental problem that every tokenization project must solve, and most fail to solve it adequately: how does an investor know that the physical asset backing the token actually exists, is in the condition claimed, and remains that way over time? Without a credible answer to this question, a token is not an investment. It is a speculation based on trust in the issuer's word.

Assetium is the independent verification layer that answers this question with objective, auditable evidence. Assetium is not owned by ARDEWA. It is an independent Third-Party Service Provider that the Client engages directly under a separate contract. ARDEWA's role is limited to facilitating the introduction and coordinating the verification workflow within the overall project timeline.

Assetium executes a multi-phase verification program. Phase A processes satellite imagery (via MKGT, a UAE Space Agency-licensed provider) and cross-references it with Luxembourg land registry records, cadastral data, MLS comparable data, and title documentation. This phase takes 72 to 96 hours and produces a preliminary geological and documentary report. Phase B deploys terrestrial LiDAR scanning (Leica precision 3D scanning) executed by a licensed EU inspection partner. The Geo-Sentinel engine captures exact floor area, structural condition, finish quality, and compliance with declared specifications. The inspection report is generated independently of any data provided by the asset owner. Phase C publishes the Digital Twin: an immutable Trust Score (0 to 100) and verified NAV for each property and for the aggregate pool on the XRP Ledger blockchain. Once published, these values cannot be modified by any party.

The verification is further reinforced by a RICS-accredited surveyor who certifies the LiDAR methodology and the NAV calculation as compliant with international property valuation standards. This certification is included as an appendix to the Whitepaper submitted to VARA. Additionally, a Big 4 firm (Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, or EY Dubai office) conducts a six-monthly audit of the data chain from physical inspection through to XRP Ledger publication. The first audit occurs before VARA submission. Subsequent audits occur every six months throughout the basket life.

Without Assetium, a tokenized real estate basket is a digital representation of a promise. With Assetium, it is a digital representation of verified reality. This is the difference that institutional investors require before committing capital. It is the piece that makes the entire model investable.

All distributions are executed automatically by the smart contract on Day 15 of each month, reading NAV data from the Assetium Oracle on the XRP Ledger.

04 / Scaling the Basket

Adding New Assets · The 80% Cost Advantage

One of the most powerful features of the ARDEWA Basket model is its scalability. Once the first USD 100M basket is structured, the legal architecture, the Whitepaper, the Smart Contract, the VARA approval, and all service provider relationships are already in place. Adding new assets to the basket is not a new project. It is an expansion of an existing regulated structure.

This means that adding USD 10,000,000 in new assets costs approximately 80% less than the initial setup. The ARDEWA Implementation Fee is already paid. The VARA submission is already approved. The legal architecture is already built. Only minor addenda to existing documents are needed. The Smart Contract requires only a minor update to incorporate the new assets. The Big 4 audit requires only a supplement, not a full new audit.

Assetium charges a reduced fee of USD 3,000 per new asset for verification, compared to the full basket verification fee. This is because the verification infrastructure (satellite processing, LiDAR coordination, Digital Twin publication pipeline) is already deployed and operational.

New tokens are minted at the updated NAV reflecting the newly added assets. Existing token holders are diluted in percentage terms but compensated by the NAV appreciation resulting from the addition of valuable new assets. Token holders vote on each new mint (simple majority required), providing governance protection. This mechanism is identical to Tranche 2 and Tranche 3 in the standard ARDEWA multi-tranche model.
